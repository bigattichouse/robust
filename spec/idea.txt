Using out taguchi tool as a template, build a bigger project that includes morris, sobol and taguchi as a series of individual tools. so we get a taguchi/ morris/ and sobol/ to help design our robust experiments for Sensitivity Analysis:

tool called 'robust' as a goal... be ideal as one tool, but could also just corrdinate three or more tools in unix style.

very common workflow is:

Morris screening
eliminate 70–90% of variables.
Sobol analysis
run on the handful that remain.
Optimization (Taguchi, Bayesian optimization, evolutionary algorithms, etc.)
find the best settings.

So they are less competitors than stages of maturity.

Taguchi asks, "How do I make this work?"

Morris asks, "What matters?"

Sobol asks, "How much does each thing matter, including the hidden interactions?"

Once you see them that way, the three methods fit together into a fairly coherent toolkit rather than three unrelated statistical techniques.
